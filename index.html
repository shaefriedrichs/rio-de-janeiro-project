Why Rio de Janeiro?

Rio, the city of samba and God

Rio de Janeiro is one of the most visited cities in the Southern Hemisphere and is known for its carnivals, samba, and incredible natural settings. In addition its nature, some of the most famous landmarks include the giant statue of Christ the Redeemer atop Corcovado mountain, named one of the New Seven Wonders of the World; Sugarloaf Mountain with its cable car; the Sambódromo, a permanent grandstand-lined parade avenue which is used during Carnival.

The city was also the host of the 2016 Summer Olympics and the 2016 Summer Paralympics, making it the first South American and Portuguese-speaking city to ever host the events, and the third time the Olympics were held in a Southern Hemisphere city.

My favourite attractions in Rio

The Selaron Steps

The Selaron Steps, or Escadaria Selarón in Portuguese, is a set of 215 steps created by Chilean artist Jorge Selarón, who covered them with over 2,000 tiles collected from over 60 countries. The colorful tiles create a stunning mosaic that attracts visitors from around the world. It has been featured in numerous movies, music videos, and advertisements, and is a popular spot for selfies.

Sugarloaf Mountain

Sugarloaf Mountain, or Pão de Açúcar in Portuguese, is a granite peak rising 396 meters above the harbor of Rio de Janeiro. The mountain is located at the enterance of Guanabara Bay and offers panoramic views of the city, the bay, and the surrounding mountains. The easiest way to reach the summit is by taking a cable car ride that offers spectacular views along the way.

Copacabana Beach

Copacabana Beach is one of the most famous beaches in the world. It stretches for 4.5 kilometers and is divided into several sections, each with its own unique vibe. Visitors can enjoy sunbathing, swimming, surfing, and beach volleyball, as well as bars, restaurants, and cafes. The beach is also known for its iconic black and white mosaic promenade, which is a popular spot for jogging and cycling.
